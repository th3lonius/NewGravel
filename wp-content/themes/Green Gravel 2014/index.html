Festival Info

The Green Gravel Comedy Festival is a three-day alternative comedy festival in multiple venues in central Iowa. Offering affordable, accessible shows and workshops in alternative comedy, Green Gravel aims to bring the finest comedic voices to the heart of Iowa. Our fest is made possible by the community of central Iowa in conjunction with sponsors, partnerships and volunteer contributions. It’s time. Because Iowa laughs harder.

We’re proud to bring an array of the finest stand-up, podcast, improv and sketch comedians to central Iowa, and we don’t believe that patrons should have to pay an arm and a leg to do it. That’s why we’re offering affordable options for ticketing: you can pop in for a single show, buy a day pass or snap up the Green Graveller weekend pass. You won’t be getting comedy-clubbed to death by minimum-drink charges; furthermore, we’ll be offering drink/merch specials as a thank-you to the folks who make the gravel strip comedy trip!

Venues

  • The Wieting Theatre
    101 S Church St, Toledo, IA 52342

    The lovely Wieting Theater serves as the primary venue for Green Gravel. The Wieting has brought fine arts ranging from live opera to 3D movies to the Toledo region since 1912. This 300-seat balcony-tiered gem has been restored and maintained by the community, showcasing the tireless work and cultural heritage of the people of Toledo. We’re honored to host our event here and can’t wait to share this completely unique live, hilarious experience with you!

  • Bumblebee's
    112 N Broadway St, Toledo, IA 52342

    Just down the block from The Wieting Theater, across from the Tama County courthouse, sits a local institution for gourmet pizza, quality karaoke and late-night spirits for locals and visitors alike. This first-generation pool hall has been the hitching post for many a weary worker for over 2 decades and is owned/operated with pride by the Musgrave family. Bumblebee’s will be hosting comedy events in conjunction with those at the Wieting and the American Legion Post 72.

  • American Legion Post 72
    109 N Main St, Toledo, IA 52342

    Located just across the town square from both The Wieting Theater and Bumblebee's, this building has been housing veteran and/or family activities for over thirty years. Boasting a full bar with numerous activity spaces, this Legion Hall will be the epicenter for our workshops, group shows and other distinct performances! We're thrilled to be curating some of our very best work in a space that has meant so much to the Toledo community.

Workshops

As a festival, we take pride in having the opportunity to empower our attendees. Sure, watching the best comedians in Iowa and beyond is great, but we want our participants to be learning about comedic techniques so that they can be producing comedy in their own communities. We are invested in building comedy by Iowans, for Iowans.

We’re going to be offering brief workshops in stand-up, sketch and improv comedy as taught by masters of the craft. Furthermore, we’re proud to be offering these at a reasonable, affordable rate. We love you guys. Come out and learn with us!

“The History and Evolution of Stand-up” - Mat Alano-Martin (Limestone Comedy Fest) “Improv Into Sketch” - Kevin McDonald (The Kids in the Hall) “Everyday Improv” - George Strader (The Black List)


You can register for classes at our TIKLY page! Hurry, as space is limited!

Submissions

There are a number of venues that showcase comedy in Iowa - from bars to comedy clubs to talent shows. Our goal is to give a voice to the performers that have yearned to grace those stages as much as those who have been prominently featured there. We welcome diverse submissions in the realm of stand-up, podcasts, sketch and improv comedy! This is your chance to showcase your very finest work!

The Green Gravel Comedy fest will be a weekend of curated comedy madness running from March 28-30, 2014! Boasting screenings, live stand-up, improv, podcast and sketch material, Toledo will be bustling with fans and creators alike. We look forward to facilitating a platform for comedy creators to share their work while networking, partying and influencing each other in ways previously unimaginable!

For specifics on submitting to Green Gravel, please visit the “Notes” section of our beloved Facebook page.

Staff

Lee Keeler
Festival President

Born and raised in Sioux City, Lee is a writer and producer living in LA. He received his MFA in Dramatic Writing with honors from the Savannah College of Art and Design. He has written for ScreenJunkies.com, The Devastator Quarterly, Crave Comedy Online and more; this work has found its way to The A.V. Club and The Huffington Post. Keeler produces and hosts The Hour of Power Hour, a monthly comedy variety event at the Improv Hollywood that regularly sells out. The show has consistently boasted some of the finest voices in comedy today, including TJ Miller, Kyle Kinane, David Spade, Greg Proops, Paul Scheer, Maria Bamford and more.

Matthew Mesaros
Vice President

Matthew hails from Savannah, Georgia but has made his home in Iowa City, Iowa as a web developer, writer, designer and pool junkie. He currently attends the University of Iowa pursuing a BFA in Graphic Design while working for Epiphany Design Co, a local web development startup. He has written film reviews for the local Iowa City monthly "Little Village" and he is also the founder, designer and curator of Cinelogue, an online film essay journal. He is a dedicated Atlanta Braves fan and occasionally performs pieces for 12 string guitar around town.

Evan Watkins
Festival Coordinator

Ohio born Evan Watkins is a comedy content producer based in LA. He's quickly made a name for himself producing content for The Laugh Factory comedy club's online markets. He's had a hand in creating various sketches, podcasts and stand-up clips, working alongside comedians such as Dom Irrera, Adam Ray, and Finesse Mitchell. He wrote and produced a year-long run of a successful standup and sketch show called "Comedy Slice" at the Hollywood Improv club. He is currently a sketch comedy writer and performer at the Improv Olympic West Theater, an avid hockey fan and podcaster. His Pittsburgh themed Yinzcast can be found on iTunes.

Derek Eadon
Festival Coordinator

Derek Eadon is a political and public affairs strategist from West Des Moines, IA. Derek has worked for political campaigns, non-profits, and large businesses. He specializes in organization building, integrated planning, and event management. Derek has led efforts on hundreds of events including: the 2013 Harkin Steak Fry, Barack Obama’s 2009 Inauguration, and Barack Obama’s 2012 3-day bus tour of Iowa.

Sarah Cho
Festival Coordinator

Sarah Cho is a playwright, filmmaker, and humorist from Santa Barbara, California. Her plays include Koreans Eat Dog, Life of the Experiment, and Monopoly, which was produced at the University of Iowa in 2014. In 2008, her film Mustache Maria received the Audience Choice Award at the Reel Loud Film Festival and premiered at the Los Angeles Women’s International Film Festival. When she is not writing plays, she is touring Iowa with The Janice Ian Experience, an improv group she started in Iowa City, or recording Beckett’s Babies, her annoying millennial podcast with Sam Collier, who is the coolest. Cho holds a BA in Theater Arts and Film and Media Studies from the University of California Santa Barbara, and she is currently pursuing her MFA in Playwriting as an Iowa Arts Fellow at the University of Iowa's Playwrights Workshop.
